Primary Legislation

Electoral Code of the Republic of Moldova

Codul Electoral (Law No. 1381-XIII of 21.11.1997, with subsequent amendments)

The Electoral Code is the primary law governing all electoral processes in Moldova. It establishes the rules for organizing elections, registering candidates, conducting campaigns, and monitoring results.

Art. 46
Prohibition on use of public resources. Public authorities, state institutions, and local authorities are prohibited from using public resources — including premises, equipment, personnel, and funds — for electoral campaign purposes in favor of any candidate or party.
Art. 47
Equal access to media. Audiovisual media outlets are required to provide equal access to all electoral candidates. Paid political advertising must be clearly labeled and limited to designated periods.
Art. 38
Voter secrecy. The secrecy of the vote is guaranteed. Any attempt to determine how a person voted, or any coercion related to voting choice, is strictly prohibited.
Art. 53
Campaign finance transparency. All political parties and electoral candidates are required to declare sources of campaign funding to the Central Electoral Commission within established deadlines. Undeclared or foreign funding is prohibited.
Key principle: The Electoral Code prohibits any form of direct or indirect pressure on voters, including promises of material benefits, threats of job loss, or use of administrative leverage to influence voting behavior.
Criminal Liability

Criminal Code — Electoral Offenses

Codul Penal al Republicii Moldova (Law No. 985-XV of 18.04.2002)

The Criminal Code defines electoral offenses as serious crimes that can result in imprisonment, fines, and loss of civil rights. These provisions apply to voters, candidates, officials, and intermediaries alike.

Art. 181
Obstruction of the exercise of electoral rights. Preventing citizens from exercising their right to vote or be elected through violence, deception, or threats is punishable by a fine or up to 3 years imprisonment.
Art. 182
Falsification of electoral documents. Falsifying voter lists, ballots, results protocols, or other electoral documents is punishable by 2–7 years imprisonment.
Art. 183
Vote buying. Offering, promising, or giving money, gifts, or other material advantages to influence voting behavior constitutes vote buying and is punishable by a fine or up to 3 years imprisonment. Receiving such benefits is also a criminal offense.
Art. 325
Bribery of public officials. When electoral manipulation involves bribing officials — including electoral commission members or local authorities — charges under the general bribery provisions may apply, carrying penalties of up to 10 years imprisonment.
Anti-Corruption

Law on Prevention and Combating Corruption

Law No. 82 of 25.05.2017

This law establishes the institutional framework for combating corruption in public life, including within electoral processes. It empowers the National Anti-Corruption Center (CNA) to investigate cases of administrative or financial manipulation.

Art. 7
Conflict of interest. Public officials must declare and recuse themselves from decisions where they have a personal interest. Electoral officials who use their position to benefit a political party are in violation of this provision.
Art. 14
Integrity testing and reporting. Citizens and organizations are encouraged to report corruption through designated channels. Whistleblower protections apply to those who report in good faith.
Reporting channel: Corruption-related electoral violations can be reported to the National Anti-Corruption Center (CNA) at cna.md or by calling the free hotline 0 800 55500.
Regulatory Body

Central Electoral Commission Regulations

Comisia Electorală Centrală (CEC) — cec.md

The CEC is Moldova's primary electoral authority. It is responsible for organizing elections, registering candidates, overseeing campaign finance, and adjudicating complaints about electoral violations.

→ Complaints about electoral violations can be submitted directly to the CEC during electoral periods.
→ The CEC monitors campaign finance declarations and can sanction parties for violations.
→ The Audiovisual Council (CCA) works alongside the CEC to monitor media compliance during campaigns.
→ Electoral observers — both domestic and international — are accredited by the CEC and have the right to monitor all stages of the electoral process.
International Framework

International Standards & Conventions

Applicable to Moldova as a signatory state

OSCE / ODIHR
Guidelines for Reviewing a Legal Framework for Elections

OSCE's Office for Democratic Institutions and Human Rights provides electoral monitoring and has issued recommendations specific to Moldova following each major election.

Venice Commission
Code of Good Practice in Electoral Matters

Establishes European standards for free and fair elections including principles of universal suffrage, equal suffrage, free suffrage, secret ballot, and direct suffrage.

UN — ICCPR
International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights, Art. 25

Every citizen has the right to vote in genuine periodic elections which shall be by universal and equal suffrage and shall be held by secret ballot, guaranteeing the free expression of the will of the electors.

Council of Europe
ECHR — Article 3 of Protocol 1

The High Contracting Parties undertake to hold free elections at reasonable intervals by secret ballot under conditions which will ensure the free expression of the opinion of the people.
