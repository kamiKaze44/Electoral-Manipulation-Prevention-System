Primary Legislation

Electoral Code of the Republic of Moldova

Codul Electoral (Law No. 326 of 08.12.2022, in force since 2023)

The new Electoral Code, adopted in 2022, is the primary law governing all electoral processes in Moldova. It established stronger mechanisms to prevent fraud, ensure transparent campaign financing, and block the misuse of public resources.

General Principles
Free and Secret Voting. The state guarantees the free expression of citizens' will. The secrecy of the vote is guaranteed. Any attempt to determine how a person voted, or any coercion related to voting choice, is strictly prohibited.
Definitions (Art. 1)
Administrative Resources. Human, financial, and material resources controlled by public officials. The Code explicitly forbids using the public budget, institutional equipment, or public personnel during working hours to favor any candidate.
Campaign Finance
Financial Transparency. All political parties and electoral candidates must transparently report all campaign funding to the CEC. Undeclared funding, anonymous donations, or financing from foreign states and criminal groups are strictly prohibited and result in immediate sanctions or candidate deregistration.
Key principle: The 2022 Electoral Code strictly separates the state from political campaigns and ensures equal opportunities for all candidates.
Criminal Liability

Criminal Code — Electoral Offenses

Codul Penal al Republicii Moldova (Law No. 985-XV of 18.04.2002, with recent severe amendments)

The Criminal Code defines electoral offenses as serious crimes. Recent amendments have heavily tightened the penalties, especially targeting organized vote-buying networks and illegal political financing.

Art. 181
Obstruction of electoral rights. Preventing citizens from voting or obstructing electoral bodies through violence, deception, or threats is punishable by a severe fine (550-750 conventional units), community service, or up to 3 years imprisonment.
Art. 181¹
Vote buying (Active Corruption). Offering, promising, or giving money, goods, services, or other advantages to a voter to exercise their rights in a certain way is punishable by a heavy fine or 1 to 5 years imprisonment.
Art. 181²
Illegal financing of political parties/campaigns. The acceptance of financing from organized criminal groups or foreign entities carries severe penalties, ranging up to 7 years imprisonment and bans on holding certain public functions.
Art. 182
Falsification of electoral documents. Falsifying voter lists, ballots, results protocols, or voting multiple times is punishable by fines, community service, or up to 5 years imprisonment.
Administrative Liability

Contravention Code — Passive Corruption

Codul Contravențional al Republicii Moldova

In response to massive electoral bribery networks, Moldova recently introduced strict administrative penalties for citizens who *accept* bribes in exchange for their vote.

Art. 47¹
Passive Electoral Corruption. Claiming, accepting, or receiving money, goods, or services by a voter to vote or not vote for a specific candidate is heavily sanctioned. The penalty consists of a massive administrative fine (up to tens of thousands of MDL). Note: Voters who self-denounce and cooperate with law enforcement can be exempted from this fine.
Anti-Corruption

Anti-Corruption Legal Framework

Electoral corruption is a primary target of specialized law enforcement bodies. The National Anti-Corruption Center (CNA) and the Anti-Corruption Prosecution Office (PA) are empowered to investigate complex networks of financial manipulation, illicit party financing, and money laundering.

Reporting channel: Electoral corruption, vote-buying schemes, and illicit campaign financing should be immediately reported to the National Anti-Corruption Center (CNA) at cna.md or by calling the national free hotline 0 800 55500.
Regulatory Body

Central Electoral Commission Regulations

Comisia Electorală Centrală (CEC) — cec.md

The CEC is Moldova's permanent, independent electoral authority responsible for ensuring the legality, transparency, and correct administration of elections.

→ The CEC monitors campaign finance declarations and can sanction parties by withholding state subsidies or requesting their removal from the race.
→ Complaints regarding administrative resources or media access can be filed to the CEC or local electoral councils.
→ Starting in 2026 (per the 2022 reform), the CEC consists of 7 members to reduce political affiliation suspicions.
